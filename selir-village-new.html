Honest answers

The questions every smart investor asks.

If yours isn't here, just ask. We'd rather lose a deal than rush an under-informed one.

Is the 14% ROI guaranteed?

No — and any operator who guarantees a return on a hospitality asset is misleading you. The 14% is a projection based on our existing units' 75% occupancy and AUD $250 nightly rate. We share the full sensitivity model: even at 60% occupancy, the projected ROI is still ~10%.

Can a foreigner actually own land in Indonesia?

Yes — through a properly structured PT-PMA (foreign-owned company) holding HGB title, which is the standard, legally recognised vehicle for foreign property investment. We handle the entire setup with our local + Australian legal counsel. Title is in your name; the structure is fully transferable.

What happens if construction is delayed?

Construction milestones are tracked and audited monthly. Capital is only released against verified milestones. In the unlikely event of delay beyond 6 months past the contract date, you're entitled to a full refund of paid-in capital plus a 5% delay fee, paid from escrow. This is written into the contract.

How do I exit?

Three paths: (1) hold and keep collecting nightly distributions, (2) sell the unit on the secondary market — we maintain a buyer waitlist, (3) trigger our optional Year-5 buyback at original price + 10%. Exit terms are spelled out in the SPA.

Can I really stay there myself?

Yes. You get up to 28 nights / year of personal use, blocked out via our investor dashboard. Outside of that, the unit is on the booking calendar generating revenue.

What's the minimum investment?

One full villa unit at AUD $250,000 (current pre-launch pricing). We do not offer fractional ownership at this stage to keep ownership and tax structure clean.

What about taxes?

You pay Indonesian rental income tax (~10% final) at source, then declare net income in your home country. Most investors offset against AU/NZ/SG income via existing double-tax treaties. We provide audited annual statements your accountant can use directly.
